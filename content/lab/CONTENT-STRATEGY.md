# lab.promptengines.com — Content Strategy

**Site:** https://lab.promptengines.com
**Purpose:** AI/tech publication. Stay current. Publish consistently.
**Content goal:** Be the go-to source for practical AI news and analysis for builders and early adopters.

---

## Content Types

### 1. News Write-ups
**What:** Take a source article (sent by you) and write a lab-voice version.
**Tone:** Sharp, opinionated, practical. "Here's what this means for you."
**Format:**
- What happened (1 paragraph)
- Why it matters (2–3 paragraphs)
- What to do about it / what to watch (1 paragraph)
- Source credit + link

### 2. Tool Reviews
**What:** Hands-on review of new AI tools relevant to the audience.
**Tone:** Honest. Say what sucks as well as what works.

### 3. Deep Dives
**What:** Longer analysis pieces on a trend, model, or technique.
**Tone:** Authoritative but not academic.

### 4. PromptEngines Experiments Cross-posts
**What:** Surface relevant experiments from promptengines.com to lab audience.

---

## Workflow
1. You send Andy a source article or topic via Telegram
2. Andy drops source in `queue/` and drafts in `drafts/`
3. Draft ready → Andy flags for your review
4. Approved → moves to `published/` + you post to site

---

## Publishing Cadence Target
- **Minimum:** 2 articles/week
- **Goal:** 4–5 articles/week once workflow is running smoothly

---

## Voice
- Direct, no fluff
- Slightly opinionated — take a position
- Assumes reader is technical or AI-curious
- Short paragraphs, scannable
- No "In conclusion" or "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape" filler
