# promptengines.com — Content Strategy

**Site:** https://promptengines.com
**Purpose:** Hub for all PromptEngines products and experiments
**Content goal:** Show the work. Build credibility. Drive interest in the products.

---

## Content Types

### 1. Experiments
**What:** Write-ups of AI experiments conducted while building the products.
**Tone:** Technical but accessible. "Here's what we tried, here's what happened."
**Triggers:** Any commit that adds a new AI feature, tests a model, or explores a new approach.
**Format:**
- What we were trying to do
- How we approached it
- What worked / what didn't
- What we learned
- Link to the product that came out of it

**Examples:**
- "We tested 6 image models for children's book illustration — here's what we found"
- "How we built a character consistency system for AI-generated storybooks"
- "Why we switched from [Model A] to [Model B] for story generation"

---

### 2. Project Updates
**What:** Major milestones, new features, launches for any PromptEngines product.
**Tone:** Founder voice. Honest, direct, excited where appropriate.
**Triggers:** Major commits, version bumps, new feature launches, product milestones.
**Format:**
- What changed
- Why it matters
- What's next

**Examples:**
- "StoryBook Studio now supports 34 art styles — here's the story behind that"
- "We just shipped print-on-demand. Here's how it works."
- "Lab.promptengines is live"

---

### 3. Project Spotlights
**What:** Deep dives into individual products in the ecosystem.
**Tone:** Product-focused, shows depth.
**Triggers:** New product launches, major redesigns, significant user milestones.
**Format:**
- What the product does
- Why we built it
- How it works (technical depth optional)
- Where it's going

---

## Content Calendar Approach
Andy monitors the repo continuously. When a significant commit lands:
1. Classify it (experiment / update / project)
2. Draft a post in the appropriate folder
3. Flag it for review via Telegram

No content goes live without approval.

---

## Voice & Tone
- **Builder's voice** — we're showing the work, not polishing a press release
- **Honest** — what didn't work is as interesting as what did
- **Specific** — name the models, show the numbers, share the real findings
- **Accessible** — technical enough to be credible, simple enough for non-engineers
