Zen and the Art of Vibe Coding

Vibe-coding gives you infinite leverage. That leverage will eat you alive without a system.

The Problem

You can build anything now. Products that required teams flow from a single prompt. The result is a new bipolarity:

Pole 1: You can build anything.
Pole 2: You feel like you're building nothing fast enough.

Every idle second becomes opportunity cost. Every moment not producing feels wasted. This is the psychological tax of high leverage — and it compounds.

The System

Five rules. Each one is a boundary that protects output quality and mental health simultaneously.

01 Major work at the computer only

No building on your phone. No "just trying something" in a note app. Build at the desk with full context. Think away from it. Mixing fragments mental loops you can't close.

02 One screen, one project

Four screens. Each locked to a project. No tab drift. No "just checking." The screen enforces the boundary.

03 Away = inputs only

Away from the workstation: research, writing, planning. Inputs that refill, not outputs that deplete. Choose things you're excited about — not obligations.

04 Phone matched to context

Walking → research, reading. Social → quick notes only, never pull out of conversation. Transit → drafts, low-stakes review.

05 Two-hour daily disconnect

Non-negotiable. Queue agent tasks. Document project states. Set DND. Leave devices. Agents work without you. You recover without them.

Why It Works

  • Screens and locations enforce context — no willpower required
  • Projects get built or deliberately paused — no hanging threads
  • Away-work is input, not fragmented output
  • The disconnect is designed, not accidental — agents queue, you leave

The leverage is real. So is the cost. Design boundaries or the leverage designs your burnout.
