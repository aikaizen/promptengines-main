## 📚 THE FOUR PILLARS

### Pillar 1: Reading — "Teach Your Child to Read in 100 Easy Lessons"

**Source**: Siegfried Engelmann, Phyllis Haddox, Elaine Bruner  
**Published**: 1986 (revised editions since)  
**Method**: Direct Instruction (DI)

#### What It Is
- 100 scripted lessons, 20 minutes/day
- Explicit phonics instruction
- Error correction built into script
- Parent/teacher reads script, child responds
- No pictures (prevents guessing), pure decoding focus

#### Why It Works
- **Project Follow Through**: Largest federal education study ever — DI outperformed all other models
- **Explicit instruction**: No "discovery learning" — direct teaching
- **Mastery-based**: Don't proceed until current skill solid
- **Scripted**: Reduces teacher variability — anyone can teach it well

#### Structure
| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sound introduction | New phonemes introduced explicitly |
| Blending practice | Sound-by-sound word reading |
| Story reading | Controlled vocabulary texts |
| Comprehension | Questions to check understanding |

#### AI Integration Opportunities
- [ ] **Adaptive pacing**: Speed up/slow down based on mastery signals
- [ ] **Pronunciation feedback**: Speech recognition for phoneme accuracy
- [ ] **Error pattern analysis**: Track which sounds trip up each child
- [ ] **Parent coaching**: Guide parents on error correction tone/technique

#### Research Needed
- [ ] Lesson script analysis (lessons 1, 25, 50, 75, 100)
- [ ] Error correction protocol documentation
- [ ] Integration with book reading (when to transition)
- [ ] Licensing: Can we reference/use structure?

### Pillar 4: Knowledge — Ed Hirsch Core Knowledge Series

**Source**: E.D. Hirsch Jr., Core Knowledge Foundation  
**Series**: "What Your [Kindergartner/First Grader/etc] Needs to Know"  
**Philosophy**: Cultural literacy through explicit content knowledge

#### What It Is
- Grade-by-grade knowledge expectations
- Domains: Literature, History, Geography, Science, Arts, Math
- Specific content, not vague skills
- "What educated people should know"

#### Why It Works
- **Cultural literacy**: Shared reference points enable communication
- **Knowledge builds on knowledge**: Prior year enables next year
- **Anti-skill-creep**: Focus on actual content, not "critical thinking" devoid of facts
- **Teacher clarity**: Teachers know exactly what to teach

#### Structure (Per Grade)
**Kindergarten**:
- Literature: Nursery rhymes, fables, fairy tales
- History/Geography: Basic concepts, community
- Science: Seasons, animals, plants
- Arts: Classical music, art appreciation

**Grade 4** (example expansion):
- Literature: Robin Hood, Pollyanna, myths
- History: Middle Ages, Knights/Castles
- Science: Electricity, geology
- Arts: Beethoven, van Gogh

#### AI Integration Opportunities
- [ ] **Knowledge assessments**: Check what child knows
- [ ] **Gap identification**: Missing Grade 2 content? Fill before Grade 3
- [ ] **Content recommendations**: Books, videos, activities to fill gaps
- [ ] **Spaced review**: Revisit prior year content periodically
- [ ] **Parent dashboard**: Visual map of knowledge domains

- [ ] **Identify critical content**: What must be memorized vs. exposed?

## 🎨 PRODUCT CONCEPT: Flow Education

### Positioning
**For**: Parents who want rigorous education, not edutainment  
**Against**: Everything-apps that do nothing well  
**Promise**: "The four curricula that actually work, combined"

### Key Differentiators
1. **Curriculum-specific**: Not generic "reading" — 100 Lessons methodology
2. **Mastery-based**: No moving on until ready
3. **Parent-respecting**: No gamification, no manipulation
4. **Knowledge-building**: Explicit content, not vague skills
5. **Integrated**: Four pillars work together, not siloed apps

### Target User
- Parent (usually mother) aged 30-45
- College-educated
- Values education, dissatisfied with public school quality
- Willing to invest 30-60 min/day in child's learning
- Rejects screen-time guilt — wants purposeful screen use
- Probably homeschools or supplements school

### Child Interface Principles
- Clean, minimal design
- No cartoons, no mascots, no rewards
- Progress shown through mastery, not points
- Reading real books, not leveled readers with artificial constraints
- Math problems that look like math, not games

### Parent Dashboard
- Progress across all 4 pillars
- Specific skill gaps highlighted
- "What to do today" recommendation
- "What you're doing well" reassurance
- "What needs attention" alerts
