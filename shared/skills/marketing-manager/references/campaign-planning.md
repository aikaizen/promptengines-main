# Campaign Planning Reference

## Campaign Types

### Launch Campaign
**Goal:** First wave of real customers + social proof
**Duration:** 4–6 weeks
**Components:**
1. Organic content seeding (2 weeks before)
2. Influencer outreach + free product sends
3. PR pitch to 5–10 publications
4. Reddit/community seeding
5. Email list building via lead magnet
6. Social announcement posts

### Seasonal Campaign
**Goal:** Ride gift-buying spikes
**Key seasons:** Christmas, Mother's Day, Father's Day, Valentine's Day, Back to School
**Lead time:** Start planning 6 weeks before, content 3 weeks before, ads 2 weeks before
**Budget multipliers:** Christmas 5x, Mother's Day 3x, others 1.5–2x

### Always-On Campaign
**Goal:** Steady acquisition between seasons
**Components:** SEO content, social posting, retargeting, email nurture

### Event Campaign
**Goal:** Local awareness, demo experience, UGC
**Components:** Event presence, live demo, email capture, follow-up sequence

## Creative Framework

### Hook Formula (Video)
- **Problem:** "I always struggle finding [X]..."
- **Curiosity:** "You're not going to believe what this app does..."
- **Reaction:** "Watch what happens when she sees this..."
- **Speed:** "I made this in 3 minutes..."
- **Comparison:** "Instead of spending $40 on Wonderbly..."

### Copy Formula (Written)
- **Headline:** Lead with the transformation/outcome, not the product
- **Body:** Problem → Agitation → Solution → Proof → CTA
- **CTA:** Action-oriented, specific ("Create your book" not "Click here")

### Visual Hierarchy
1. Hook image/frame stops the scroll
2. Product in use (not product alone)
3. Emotion (child's face, parent reaction)
4. CTA visible without sound

## Measurement Framework

### Weekly Metrics
- Traffic (sessions, new users)
- Social followers + engagement rate
- Email list growth
- Content pieces published

### Monthly Metrics
- Conversion rate (visitor → book created)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Revenue + average order value (AOV)
- ROAS per channel
- Email open/click rates

### Quarterly Metrics
- SEO ranking improvements
- Organic traffic growth %
- Paid vs organic revenue split
- LTV (lifetime value) per customer
- NPS / customer satisfaction

## Budget Allocation Framework

### Early Stage (Pre-product-market fit)
- 80% organic (content, community, influencer gifting)
- 15% paid (small tests, $15–20/day max)
- 5% events/PR

### Growth Stage (Proven conversion rate)
- 40% paid social (Meta + TikTok)
- 20% Google/search
- 20% content/SEO
- 10% influencer/PR
- 10% events

### Scale Stage
- 50% paid (proven ROAS)
- 25% content/SEO (compounding)
- 15% events/partnerships
- 10% PR/influencer

## Austin Go-To-Market Specifics

### Why Austin Matters
- 2M+ metro population, fast-growing
- Heavy tech/startup culture = early adopters
- Strong parenting community (high birth rate, family-oriented suburbs)
- SXSW = global press in one place every March
- Texas Book Festival = book-focused audience (40k+ attendees, October)

### Austin Launch Sequence
1. **Month 1:** Soft presence — seed with Austin parent Facebook/Reddit groups
2. **Month 2:** Reach out to Austin Moms Blog, Austin Family Magazine for feature
3. **Month 3:** SXSW presence if timing aligns — demo booth or side event
4. **Month 4:** Texas Book Festival booth (October) — perfect audience fit
5. **Ongoing:** BookPeople partnership, Austin Public Library storytime integration

### Local Press Targets
- Austin American-Statesman
- Austin Monthly
- Austin Moms Blog (austinmomsblog.com)
- Austin Family Magazine
- KUT / KUTX (NPR Austin) — tech + culture stories
- KXAN / KVUE — local TV, "cool tech" segments
- Community Impact Newspaper (suburban parents)

### Austin Community Channels
- r/Austin, r/AustinParents on Reddit
- Austin Moms Facebook Group (200k+ members)
- Nextdoor — neighborhood-level targeting
- Austin ISD parent groups
- WeAreAustin community

## Competitor Intelligence Checklist

When researching competitors, document:
- [ ] Pricing model
- [ ] Keyword rankings (top 20)
- [ ] Backlink profile (top referring domains)
- [ ] Social following + engagement rate
- [ ] Ad creative (Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center)
- [ ] Email sequence (sign up as a customer)
- [ ] Product gaps / negative reviews
- [ ] Messaging + positioning

### Current Competitors
- Wonderbly (wonderbly.com) — main competitor, fixed templates, print only
- Lost My Name — name-based personalization, limited AI
- Shutterfly — photo books, not story-focused
- Artifact Uprising — premium photo/memory books
- Etsy custom book sellers — manual, slow, expensive
