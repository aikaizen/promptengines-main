# Exceptional Education Skill

Research and prototype an educational app combining four best-in-class curriculum materials. Refuse slop. Choose the best.

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Teach Your Child to Read in 100 Easy Lessons (Engelmann)
**What it is**: Direct Instruction reading program — 20 minutes/day, 100 lessons
**Target age**: Start at 4-5 (earlier if high aptitude)
**Why it works**: Scripted lessons, error correction built-in, proven in Project Follow Through
**Integration opportunity**: AI-powered progress tracking, adaptive pacing, pronunciation feedback

### 2. FLDOE Recommended Book Lists
**What it is**: Florida Department of Education curated novels by grade level
**Structure**: Read → Discuss → Write
**Why it works**: Grade-appropriate complexity, cultural breadth, structured progression
**Integration opportunity**: AI reading comprehension checks, Socratic discussion prompts, writing assignment generation

### 3. Math Academy
**What it is**: Claims "light years ahead of any other app" for grade 4+
**Research needed**: What exactly do they do differently? (spaced repetition? mastery-based? problem types?)
**Integration opportunity**: Diagnostic assessment, personalized problem sets, explanation generation

### 4. Ed Hirsch Core Knowledge Series
**What it is**: "What Your [Kindergartner/First Grader/etc] Needs to Know" — domain knowledge curriculum
**Structure**: Grade-level expectations across literature, history, science, arts
**Why it works**: Cultural literacy approach, specific content over vague skills
**Integration opportunity**: AI-generated assessments, knowledge gap identification, personalized content recommendations

## Design Principles (Use with Anti-Slop Skill)

### Refuse Slop Manifesto
- No generic "learning games"
- No points/badges manipulation
- No watered-down content for "engagement"
- No everything-apps that do nothing well

### Exceptional Markers
| Instead of... | Build... |
|---------------|----------|
| "Adaptive learning" | Specific lesson adjustment based on error patterns |
| "Reading comprehension" | Socratic questioning tied to specific book content |
| "Math practice" | Mastery-based progression with explanation quality checks |
| "Knowledge building" | Explicit domain coverage with gap analysis |

### Parent Promise
"We combined the four curricula that actually work. Not the ones with the best marketing. Not the ones that keep kids addicted to screens. The ones that produce literate, numerate, knowledgeable children."

## Success Metrics

Not "engagement time" or "daily active users."

Real metrics:
- Reading level improvement (DIBELS, standardized)
- Math proficiency gains (state test alignment)
- Knowledge assessment (Core Knowledge checkpoints)
- Parent satisfaction on "worth the effort"
- Child can actually do things they couldn't before
